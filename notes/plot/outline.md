# Story Outline

## Overall Structure

### Prologue (Complete)
**Setup**: Unnamed narrator, drunk at 2 AM, feeds three-legged dog in alley behind Kepler's Bar
**Inciting incident**: Mara appears with two men (tall/transforming, short), demands he show them where father's key is
**Key elements established**:
- Father died 6 months ago, left brass key on leather cord
- Dr. Zheng (mentor) betrayed narrator under torture, gave up his name
- Mara: ex-lover from age 17, steady/cruel, shoots dog instead of narrator
- Dog-man: tall man transforms into Converted (dog-shaped, claws, teeth)
- Nested worlds concept from Zheng's faculty dinner story (grandfather crossed over)

**Extended memory sequence**: Faculty dinner at Paradiso restaurant
- Red/white checkered tablecloths
- Woman with too much perfume (floral, cloying, mixed with garlic bread)
- Zheng drunk, telling story of grandfather who crossed over
- Nested worlds "like Russian dolls" with keys/words/state of mind to access
- Grandfather told Zheng once when he was 16, never spoke of it again
- Jokes about Narnia, Dante; but Zheng serious, believing

**Transition to Chapter 1**: 
- They march narrator through morning streets to retrieve key
- Mara grabs his hand gruff (gun at ribs), then softens
- Dog-man obsessed with modern LED street lamps
- Mentions whale oil, killing of sperm whale, purpose for city lights
- Shorter man violent/impatient, yanking dog-man
- Dog-man freezes under lamp, scratches post, launches into story
- "We'd been tracking them for two days. The *Petrel*."

### Chapter 1 (In Progress)
**POV shift**: Now first person from dog-man's perspective (pirate, early 1800s)
**Setting**: Pirate ship tracking whaling vessel *Petrel* out of Nantucket

**Key characters introduced**:
- Narrator (dog-man): competent, middling pirate, observant, fixated on purpose
- Captain Edward Fitch: accountant's son from Manchester, spectacles, ledger, efficient violence
- Captain Silas Worth: *Petrel* captain, old man, bark-hands, weathered leather face
- Thomas: pirate gutted by harpoon, falls into red water
- Davies: fires first cannon
- Multi-cultural whaling crew: Portuguese, Polynesian, African, Irish, speaking ship-pidgin

**Plot events**:
- Six-hour whale hunt witnessed from distance
- Enormous sperm whale, multiple boats, one boat pulled under (3 crew lost)
- Whale harpooned, still alive, being butchered while breathing
- Low moaning sounds "like trying to speak"
- Pirates attack with cannons, grapples, board
- Whalers keep working obsessively, barely fight back
- Fitch shoots methodically (Polynesian with flensing knife, others)
- Narrator fights, kills two whalers
- Thomas gutted by harpoon, falls overboard into red water
- Captain Worth leaps onto whale's back, drives harpoon through own chest into whale
- Worth dies looking at whale's eye (which looks at narrator)
- 20 minutes of murder and looting
- Whale dies during looting
- Poor haul: whale oil not worth casualties
- Narrator fixated: oil for street lamps in cities, polished people walking safely

**Themes emerging**:
- Violence without meaning (murder for poor haul)
- Purpose vs meaninglessness (whale dies so distant cities have light)
- Obsession (whalers work to the end, narrator fixates on purpose)
- Methodical horror (both whale butchering and pirate killing described plainly)

## Major Themes
- Purpose and meaninglessness (what we kill for, what we burn)
- Violence rendered plainly, without romance
- Nested realities / layers of existence
- Obsession and fixation (lamps, whale, oil, purpose)
- Rationality vs irrationality in same person
- Memory as trigger for reality-shifting
- Truth through simplified, direct observation

## Structural Notes
- Dean Koontz process: perfect each page before moving forward
- Frame structures emerge organically - triggered by objects, memories, associations
- Don't force nested levels - let them develop naturally
- Philosophy revealed through action, never explicit discussion
- Each first-person narrator has distinct voice and obsessions
- Time is fluid between frames (prologue modern, Ch1 1800s)
- No explanation of mechanics - just show the shifts
